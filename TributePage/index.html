Mustafa Kemal Atatürk

The Founder of Modern Turkey

  • 1881: Mustafa Kemal Atatürk is born in Salonica (now Thessaloniki, Greece), then part of the Ottoman Empire.
  • 1893: Enrolls in the Military Preparatory School in Salonica.
  • 1896: Enrolls in the Monastir Military High School.
  • 1899: Enters the Ottoman Military Academy in Istanbul.
  • 1902: Graduates from the Ottoman Military Academy with the rank of lieutenant.
  • 1905: Completes his education at the Ottoman Military College in Istanbul and is assigned to Damascus.
  • 1908: Participates in the Young Turk Revolution which restores the constitution of 1876.
  • 1911: Fights in the Italo-Turkish War in Libya.
  • 1912: Serves in the Balkan Wars, defending the Ottoman Empire against Balkan League forces.
  • 1915: Leads Ottoman forces to a crucial victory at the Battle of Gallipoli during World War I.
  • 1919: Arrives in Samsun, beginning the Turkish War of Independence.
  • 1920: Founds the Grand National Assembly of Turkey in Ankara.
  • 1921: Wins the Battle of Sakarya, a turning point in the War of Independence.
  • 1922: Achieves victory in the Battle of Dumlupınar, effectively ending the War of Independence.
  • 1923: Proclaims the Republic of Turkey and is elected its first president.
  • 1924: Abolishes the caliphate and embarks on a series of radical reforms to modernize Turkey.
  • 1934: Given the surname "Atatürk" (Father of the Turks) by the Turkish parliament.
  • 1938: Passes away on November 10 in Istanbul.
